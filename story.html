a short bio

I was born in Algeria, and I am part of an ethnic group indigenous to Kabylia in North Africa. I grew up listening to hip-hop and even tried breakdancing while I was in middle school. By the time I reached high school, I was a full-time rock-n-roll fan. I’ve also always had an interest in computer programming and algorithmic thinking. To me, it seems like the most efficient way to go about life (there is a great book on this called Algorithms to Live By by Brian Christian and Tom Griffiths). For a brief stint I even tried studying C programming language, but I wasn’t able to stick with it at the time. So what am I up to now?

There’s this saying, “We see a reflection of ourselves in the world.” I often understand the world around me by looking at how I feel during a specific moment in time. In 2015, I graduated from Batna University with a degree in Environmental Engineering. I felt like something was missing. I kept thinking that there’s a much bigger world out there and the only way to really explore it is to take a leap of faith. That fall, I decided it was time to pursue the dreams I had put on hold and moved over 4,000 miles to the United States to make them happen.

I’ve been in the U.S. for almost three years now. Most of that time has been spent learning English, meeting some pretty incredible people, and trying foods I never knew existed. As the years went by, I knew there was more I wanted to accomplish. But I started thinking of this experience as an investment. Everyday I sat in an English language course or found my way round the city, I was learning something new that would ultimately help me achieve my bigger goals. From the little things, like learning how to swipe a metrocard on the Subway, to the bigger ones, like learning the ins and outs of a new culture, I’ve slowly but surely found my way. I now speak Algerian Arabic, French, and English. Next up, I’m finally learning the language of the machine (kinda… there’s a middleman or more accurately a middle program, compilers). For the past couple of months I’ve been studying full-stack development with Code Immersives at the Digital Film Academy. Everything is all about algorithm-this and algorithm-that (in reference to the two movies: Analyze this, and Analyze that.)... I feel like I'm finally on the right path.

One of the biggest lessons I’ve learned so far is how little I actually knew. I think it is a universal experience: to think you know a lot about something only to realize you hadn’t even scratched the surface yet. Once you start educating yourself, reading books, and reaching out to others this whole other world opens up about the matter at hand. And while it’s jarring at first to realize how much you have left to learn, I think it’s okay to confront that setback and recognize the doubts it brings with it. I’ve found that those low points often come right before a breakthrough, allowing you to gain a little confidence that will eventually accumulate. I thrive on new challenges and look forward to the next ones, where I can code my way through and sculpt a new path.

On a personal level, I want to learn and grow from my challenges, big and small. And I want to try to understand myself and figure out who I’ll become. On a larger level, I think we all have a duty to our societies and that our ultimate purpose is to contribute to and thrive in them. Now that I’m here and finally back on track, I hope to grow as a member of the community I live in and code some really cool stuff.

- Mounir B. -
